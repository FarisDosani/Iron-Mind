Squats

A foundational movement where you lower your body by bending your knees and hips while keeping your chest upright and back straight. Squats strengthen the entire lower body and core, making them a staple for building strength and stability.

Lunges

A unilateral exercise where you step forward or backward, lowering your body until both knees form 90-degree angles. Lunges improve balance, coordination, and isolate each leg to address muscle imbalances.

Deadlifts

A compound lift where you hinge at the hips to pick up and lower a barbell or dumbbells. Deadlifts build strength in the hamstrings, glutes, and lower back, essential for improving posture and overall power.

Step-Ups

This functional exercise involves stepping onto a raised platform and back down, mimicking everyday movements like climbing stairs. It targets the quadriceps, glutes, and hamstrings while enhancing balance and coordination.

Calf Raises

A simple movement where you lift your heels off the ground and balance on the balls of your feet. This isolates the calf muscles, improving strength and endurance for activities like running and jumping.
